A 58-year-old male with hypertension presents with crushing chest pain radiating to the left jaw, diaphoresis, and nausea. SpO₂ is 91%. ECG shows ST elevation in leads II, III, and aVF. What is the nurse's priority action?
Correct: C

ST elevation in II, III, aVF = inferior STEMI. SpO₂ 91% mandates immediate oxygen. Notify physician to activate STEMI protocol. Morphine use is now more conservative (A is not first). Repeating ECG (B) delays treatment. Cath prep (D) requires physician order first.

PNLE tip: Prioritization = ABC → Safety → Acute over chronic.
